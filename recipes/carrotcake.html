Angie's Ginger Carrot Cake

Description

A moist, dense cake with a little zing and crunch. I believe this recipe was adapted from a NYT recipe. Before this cake, I was grossed out by carrot cakes, but I would gladly celebrate any and every occasion with this lovely, delectable cake. And, there are carrots in it so it's healthy! ;)

Caution, this cake will take you all day to make. Maybe more experienced bakers can make it in less than 3 hours, but it has always taken me 5-6 hours from start to finish. But that's okay, it's become more about the journey for me anyways, though the destination is wildly delicious.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of peeled, graded carrots
  • 2 and 1/2 to 3 cups of sifted icing sugar (frosting)
  • 2 cups of flour
  • 2 cups of lightly packed brown sugar
  • 1 and 1/4 cups of olive oil
  • 1 and 1/4 cups of chopped, toasted pecans
  • 8 ounces of room temperature cream cheese (frosting)
  • 4 eggs
  • 6 tablespoons of room temperature, unsalted butter
  • 1/4 of a cup of dried, chopped apricots (can add extra, chopped or whole to top as decoration)
  • 1/4 of a cup of pure maple syrup (frosting)
  • 2 tablespoons of peeled, minced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of cinnamon
  • 2 teaspoons of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Butter two 9 inch diameter cake pans and line bottom with parchment paper. Butter and flour the parchment paper.
  3. In a large bowl, with an electric mixer (or vigorous whisking) beat together sugar and oil. Whisk in eggs 1 at a time.
  4. Add flour mixture and mix on low speed until well blended. Add vanilla extract.
  5. Stir in carrots, pecans, ginger and apricots.
  6. Divide batter between prepared pans and bake cakes until tester inserted into centre comes out clean, about 40 minutes.
  7. When done baking, cool cakes in pans for about 15 minutes and then turn out onto racks, peel off parchment paper, and cool completely.
  8. To make the frosting, beat together cream cheese and butter until fluffy with an electric mixer (or vigorous whisking). Then beat in the maple syrup. Finally, at a low speed, add icing sugar 1/2 cup at a time, until a thick, smooth icing forms.
  9. On chilled cake, place 1 cake layer on platter. Spread with about half of the icing. Top with second layer and spread remaining icing on top of cake. Only frost the side with a thin layer of frosting. Add any extra nuts, dried fruit, etc to the top as decoration. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and chill.
  10. Remove from refrigerator 30 minutes before serving.
